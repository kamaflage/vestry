Vestry User Guide
01
Getting Started
What happens when you open Vestry for the first time

The first time you open Vestry, a setup wizard walks you through 5 quick steps to personalise your dashboard. It only appears once — if you've already configured Vestry, it won't show again.

1
Welcome
Vestry introduces itself. Your data lives only on your device — nothing is sent anywhere.
2
Household setup
Enter your family name, your name, and a short owner tag used on share exports.
3
Monthly targets
Set your income target, expense limit, and savings target. These unlock progress bars on dashboard cards.
4
First intention
Add something you're working toward — this shows on your dashboard every time you open the app.
5
Ready
Your personalised dashboard is waiting. Add your first transaction using + Add.
💡Takes about 2 minutes. You can update everything later in the Config page — nothing is locked in.
02
Configuration
Personalise Vestry to fit your household

The Config page is your control panel. Access it from the sidebar or hamburger menu. Changes take effect immediately.

🏠
Family & Person
Your household name, personal name, and owner tag. The tag auto-stamps transactions and share exports.
🎯
Monthly Targets
Income target, expense limit, savings target. Once set, your dashboard cards show progress bars.
💡
Budget Intentions
What you're working toward. The first intention shows in your motivational banner every session.
🎨
Motivational Style
Gentle — warm tone. Coach — direct. Data — numbers only. Affects banner and health score messages.
03
Transactions
Logging income, expenses, and savings

A transaction is any money movement. Every transaction has a date, description, category, type, and amount. An optional owner tag records who logged it.

TypeWhen to use
● IncomeSalary, freelance, benefits, rental income
● ExpenseRegular spending — groceries, utilities, subscriptions
● SavingsMoney moved to savings, TFSA, investments
● One-offIrregular large purchases — appliance, car repair
💡Use Savings for TFSA and investment deposits — not Expense. Vestry's health score rewards saving behaviour specifically.

To edit a transaction, tap the ✎ pencil icon on any row. To delete, tap the ✕ button.

04
Dashboard
Reading your Overview at a glance

The four summary cards update for the selected month. Use ‹ and › to browse previous months.

CardWhat it showsProgress bar

The motivational banner greets you by name, shows your first budget intention, and gives a one-line summary of your month. Its tone adapts to your Motivational Style setting.

05
Budget Health Score
How Vestry grades your financial behaviour

The health score (0–100, shown as a letter grade) appears in the sidebar. It's a compass — not a judgment.

D
0–39
Spending significantly exceeds income.
C
40–54
Below-average savings, expenses close to income.
B
55–69
On track — savings positive, under limit.
A
70–84
Strong savings rate, well under expense limit.
A+
85–100
Savings target met, expenses well under limit.
📊How it's calculated: Starts at 50. +30 for savings rate, +20 for expenses under 70% of income, +20 for hitting targets. Deductions for overspending.
06
Savings Goals
Tracking progress toward specific milestones

A Savings Goal has a name, a target amount, your current balance, and a monthly contribution. Vestry calculates how many months until you reach the goal.

FieldExample
Goal NameEmergency Fund
Target ($)$15,000
Already Saved ($)$3,200
Monthly Contribution$400 → 29 months to goal
ℹ️Goals track a separate running total updated manually. The Saved dashboard card counts Savings-type transactions this month. Use both together.
07
Recurring Bills
Cataloguing your fixed monthly obligations

Recurring Bills is a registry of fixed monthly costs — rent, insurance, subscriptions, utilities. It shows your committed spend: the baseline you owe every month before any discretionary spending.

⚠️Recurring bills don't auto-generate transactions. They are a reference list only. When you pay a bill, also log it as an Expense transaction to keep your dashboard totals accurate.
💡Add all your fixed bills first. The Monthly Fixed total tells you the minimum you need to earn each month — that's your floor.
08
Work Hours, Mileage & Invoicing
For contractors and part-time work: log time, log driving, bill it, track it as income

The Work Hours page (sidebar) is a separate ledger for hours and mileage you plan to invoice a client or employer for. It stays separate from your household budget until you mark an invoice Paid — at that point it becomes a real Income transaction automatically.

Step 1 — Set up Config first. Go to Config → Contract Work & Invoicing and fill in your name, your client's name, your hourly wage, your fuel price per litre and vehicle fuel economy (L/100km) — Vestry works out your mileage rate from these automatically, so it stays current as gas prices change — plus your distance unit (km or mi), invoice prefix/numbering, and payment terms. These fields populate every invoice you generate.

1
+ Log Hours
Enter a date, the hours worked, and an optional note. Each entry shows as "Unbilled" until it's invoiced.
2
+ Log Mileage
Choose "Total distance driven" if you already know the number, or "Odometer readings" to enter before/after readings — Vestry subtracts them for you and keeps both readings on the record for your own mileage log.
3
+ Log Expense
For store pickups, supplies, or anything you paid for that needs to be paid back. Enter the date, store, and amount. These roll into a formal invoice the same as Hours and Mileage — or, for informal reimbursement, use "📋 Copy Summary for Range" in the Expense Log to get a paste-ready total plus a list of the stores, and mark them sent without generating a full invoice.
4
🧾 Generate Invoice
Pick a range — This Week, Last Week, This Month, Last Month, or a Custom range. Vestry pulls every un-invoiced hour, mileage entry, and expense in that range and totals them at your configured rates.
5
View, Print, or Save as PDF
The invoice opens automatically. "Print / Save PDF" opens a clean printable copy in a new tab — use your browser's print dialog and choose "Save as PDF" if you need a file to send.
6
Mark as Paid
Once the client pays, mark the invoice Paid. This creates an Income transaction dated today, using your configured category label, and it now counts in your dashboard totals and Trends.
⚠️Hours and mileage logged are not income until the invoice is marked Paid. An unpaid invoice does not affect your budget totals — this avoids counting money you haven't received yet.
💡Marking an invoice Unpaid (or deleting it) reverses everything cleanly: the income transaction is removed and the underlying hours/mileage entries go back to "Unbilled" so you can re-invoice them.

The Overview dashboard shows a Contract Work snapshot — Hours YTD, Mileage YTD, Unbilled amount, and Paid YTD — so your contract income is visible alongside the rest of your budget without digging into the Work Hours page.

09
Sharing & Merge
How couples consolidate finances

Use 📤 Share to export your transactions for a date range. Your partner uses 🔀 Merge to bring them in. No server, no account — just a JSON file sent over WhatsApp, email, or any channel.

1
Click 📤 Share
Opens the Share modal with the current month pre-filled.
2
Set date range & owner tag
Your owner tag stamps every exported transaction so your partner knows which entries are yours.
3
Export as JSON & send
File downloads automatically. Send via any channel.
4
Partner clicks 🔀 Merge
Preview shows new vs duplicates. Duplicates (same date + description + amount) are skipped automatically.
10
Backup & Restore
Protecting your data

Vestry stores everything in your browser's local storage. If you clear browser data or switch devices, your data could be lost. Back up regularly.

⬇️
Backup (Export)
Click ⬇ Backup in the topbar. Downloads a complete JSON file — all transactions, bills, goals, and config.
⬆️
Restore (Import)
Click ⬆ Restore. Choose a backup JSON. Replaces ALL current data — make sure you have a recent backup first.
💡Back up at the end of each month. Name files clearly: vestry_backup_2026-03.json
11
Import & Export CSV
Moving data in bulk

CSV import is the fastest way to get bank data into Vestry. Most banks let you export a date range as CSV from online banking.

📋Required format: Date, Description, Category, Type, Amount
Date: YYYY-MM-DD  ·  Type: income / expense / savings / one-off
Example: 2026-03-15, Loblaws, Groceries, expense, 142.50

Use Paste CSV to paste rows directly, or Import CSV to upload a file from your device.

FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions answered
Is my data stored in the cloud? ▼
No. Everything is stored in your browser's localStorage on your device only. Nothing is ever sent to any server.
Can I use Vestry offline? ▼
Yes. Once installed to your home screen, Vestry works fully offline. The service worker caches everything needed.
I accidentally deleted a transaction. Can I undo it? ▼
There is no undo in the current version. If you have a recent backup you can restore it, but that replaces all data since the backup. Best practice: back up at the end of each month.
My partner and I logged the same expense. Will it double-count? ▼
No. Merge uses duplicate detection — same date, description, and amount = one entry. The merge preview shows exactly how many duplicates will be skipped before you confirm.
How do I reset Vestry and start fresh? ▼
Open browser developer tools (F12 on desktop), go to Application → Local Storage, and delete entries starting with vestry_. Reload and the setup wizard appears again. Export a backup first!
How do I run the setup wizard again? ▼
Delete the localStorage key vestry_ob_done and reload. Your existing transactions and config are preserved — the wizard only writes to config fields.
How do I change my currency symbol? ▼
Config → Motivational Style section → Currency Symbol. Options: $, £, €, ₹. Updates everywhere in the app immediately.
Why doesn't logging hours show up as income right away? ▼
By design. Hours and mileage are unbilled until you generate an invoice, and the invoice doesn't become income until you mark it Paid. This keeps your budget reflecting money actually received, not money you're owed.
Can I enter mileage as a start/end odometer reading instead of total distance? ▼
Yes. In + Log Mileage, switch the mode to "Odometer readings" and enter before/after — Vestry calculates the distance for you and keeps both readings on file.
